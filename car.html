Income Streams

As we all know from the 10X Rule, and general common sense, passive income and constant cash flow/replenishment makes more sense than relying on the uncertainty of small month-to-month paychecks. Diversification is key, as anyone with half a brain realises. I have listed below all of the methods I personally use/have previously used to build 'wealth'. Note that I am certainly no millionaire however, standard millionaire/billionaire practice can be easily applied to smaller, more socially standard household wealth - in the thousand, ten-thousand and hundred-thousand ranges. I've also included brief notes on my personal cash management.

Passive Income

1) Stock, Commodity & Cryptocurrency Portfolio:

A well diversified folio can lead to a consistent/growing frequent cash in-flow

2) Bond & Low-Risk Asset Portfolio:

Bonds and assets alike can be as low as £5k and provide comfortable cash flow supplement at maturity

3) ISA & Savings Accounts:

Small reward but a good place to keep £10k+ sums of cash which aren't enough to invest in larger opportunities

Human Labour Income

4) Self-Employment:

Offering higher-risk, higher-return as the growth and stability of this cash flow vein is in your hands

5) Other Employment:

As referenced above, as a student, this income method provides a small but stable cash replenishment stream

6) Freelancing Talents:

Freelancing small talents such as graphic design, financial management, manual labour etc. makes a good supplement

Cash Flow Management

A) Cash In/Out Current:

Receive all personal payments and make all low-medium cash purchases. Also a hub for managing cash distribution

B) Large Cash In/Out Current:

Not holding as much as savings accounts etc. but used for larger transactions

C) Everyday Spending Current:

Used to receive budgets from myself for very small daily purchases such as train tickets, beer etc.

D) Business Account:

Used to receive personally owned business cash inflows and for business expenses - utilising business benefits

E) Credit Account:

Used for any purchase of my choice, usually larger purchases to gain the benefits of the account (and credit score)
